About the event

We are very excited to introduce the fourth annual TEDxPhillipsExeterAcademy Event, "The Edge." You will have the opportunity to hear ten incredible student speakers share their personal stories, ranging from Thai kickboxing to journaling. Join us on Friday evening to learn, discover, and get inspired!

Do NOT sign up, if you are unable to stay for the entire program (60 minutes +). If you sign up but are not at The Bowld by 7:30 pm, your seat may be given away.

In the past, we have had students speak about being a cultural outsider, Asian identity and Mulan, as well as race and genetics.

What is TEDx?


In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TED has created a program called TEDx. TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. Our event is called TEDxPhillipsExeterAcademy, where x = independently organized TED event. At our TEDxPhillipsExeterAcademy event, TED Talks video and live speakers will combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events, including ours, are self-organized.

Speakers


Nahla Owens
NAHLA OWENS

Nahla is a four-year senior from Houston, Texas by way of Atlanta, Georgia. She is a passionate advocate for racial and economic equity in education with experience in qualitative research and organizing to enact policy changes.

Growing up, my mother always quoted her alma-mater Clark Atlanta University in times of struggle (shoutout to HBCU grads). She reminded me that in life you either “find a way or make one.” To me, “The Search” is the process of finding a path to opportunity or forging one for yourself. In my own life, this has often related to a search for educational opportunity and I’ve learned so many valuable lessons along this journey that I’m thrilled to share with you!"

Dellara Sheibani
DELLARA SHEIBANI

Dellara is an Upper in Dunbar Hall who enjoys learning about astronomy and Latin. She loves hiking with her dog on snowy days.

I started a search to find meaning and belonging in my life which took me thousands of miles from home. In the end, I found my answers right back at home with my family and the ones I love.

Nathan Zhou
NATHAN ZHOU

Originally hailing from Salt Lake City, Utah, Nathan is a junior at Phillips Exeter Academy. He also enjoys skiing, playing lacrosse, and teaching math to students of all ages.

To Nathan “The Search” is to constantly seek out new perspectives and hopefully use the unique takeaways people have from these experiences to inspire change.

Siona Jain
SIONA JAIN

Siona (she/her) is a 3 year Junior studying at Phillips Exeter Academy. She hails from the suburbs of Chicago, IL with her twin sister.

To me, the “Search” means an examination of my relationship with Jainism, a small religion in India. Jainism promotes minimalism, love to all forms of love, and nonviolence to plants and animals. The way I view religion has been influenced by my surroundings in America, and in this talk, I’ll challenge the rigidity of faith and search for individual interpretation.

Achyuta Rajaram
ACHYUTA RAJARAM

Hi, I'm Achyuta (he/him/his), a prep from Soule Hall. I have studied cryptography for a while, and I am really excited to share my experiences with research with you all!

I think that the most important search is that for discovery, finding new ways to improve technology and society. For me, this "search" was in the process of conducting this research and searching for a solution and improvement. This came from finding the problem to brainstorming solutions to settling on one and flushing it out. Also, this research specifically uses "string search" as an example of a problem that can be optimized using this tool :).

Matilda Damon
MATILDA DAMON

Humans to Mars Summit Representative, Mars Generation Ambassador, SFYN North American Representative, Astronaut in training.

The search to me is the search for our place in the universe, and our search for a home beyond Earth. Our search for what makes us human and what that will mean from a different planet. The search for a future that is not plagued with global warming, pollution, and catastrophe, and is instead represented by our scientific curiosities and growth.

Kodi Suzuki-Lopez
KODI SUZUKI-LOPEZ

I was homeless with drug addicted parents in East Los Angeles. Let me take you through my firsthand experience of losing a home, parental drug abuse, and the results of sobriety and perseverance.

The theme of "The Search" reminds me of the journey my family and I made while being homeless. We searched for hope, we searched for stability, and we searched for a home.

Emma Chen
EMMA CHEN

Emma Chen is an upper from Shanghai, China who lives in Langdell on campus. She is fascinated by neuroscience and enjoys reading and running in her spare time.

The Search is more than finding what you don’t have – it’s also looking at what you have in a new way or building something new.

Aavik Wadivkar
AAVIK WADIVKAR

Aavik Wadivkar is a prep/freshman from Houston. He enjoys reading, learning more about astrophysics and philosophy, and making music and videos on his Youtube channel: Essence of Reality.

"The Search" is a universal human quest: a search for meaning, and a search for answers. Every person answers them in different ways; some turn to religion and faith, while others prefer the facts and figures of science. That difference - the gap between answers - is where the magic happens.
